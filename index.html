about

 

Silicate is an arts collective based in Reno and the Bay Area. We incorporate a number of projects and initiatives inspired by burner culture and the high desert experience. At present our main project is converting a 5-ton military truck into an art car. We also have a modest sound system for use at outdoor events. More to come as further developments materialize...

projects

 

Art car

We acquired a 5-ton M939 military truck, and we are in the process of building an upper level, as well as integrating lights, sound, and transformative design elements to create a fully mutated vehicle. We aim to have the project completed in time for Burning Man 2016. The design will be that of a whale, a nod to a scene from The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.

Tripxl

- video
Tripxl is a set of triangular LED panels and a software system designed to display evolving mathematical patterns inspired by the shape. Each module is a 4' equilateral triangle with 252 LEDs and an Arduino Uno, housed in a handmade plywood box. Two modules have been built thus far, and the software continues to evolve to support more features and patterns.

Sound system

Our sound consists of two dual 18" subs and two dual 15" tops from the JBL SRX series (these puppies can slap!). Rounding out the setup are CDJ-2000s, a DJM-800, a 7kW Honda inverter generator (quiet!), and various lights and decorations.
